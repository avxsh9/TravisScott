2. Definitions

  • Account: a registered user account on TicketAdda.
  • Buyer: a user who purchases a ticket via TicketAdda.
  • Seller: a user who lists a ticket for resale on TicketAdda.
  • Ticket: any admission or access right to an event (concert, movie, sport, theatre, etc.) listed on the Platform.
  • Original Ticket / Provider Ticket: the ticket originally issued by the primary seller/organizer (e.g., BookMyShow, promoter).
  • Platform Token / TicketAdda Token: a one-time-use digital token (QR or code) issued by TicketAdda to the Buyer after a successful sale via the Platform; the Platform Token is the only token accepted by TicketAdda-integrated validation flows for entry.
  • Escrow: funds from Buyer held by TicketAdda pending satisfaction of release conditions set out in these Terms.
  • Event Date/Time: scheduled start time and date of the event per the event organizer.

6. Purchase, Escrow & Token Issuance

Purchase: When a Buyer purchases a listed Ticket, the Buyer’s payment will be taken and held in Escrow by TicketAdda (or by our payment provider acting per our direction) until release conditions are met.

Token Issuance: TicketAdda will not forward a Seller’s raw/original provider QR/code to a Buyer. Instead, after the sale steps are complete and Seller cooperation/confirmation is satisfied, TicketAdda will issue a unique Platform Token bound to the Buyer. This Platform Token is the valid token for entry under TicketAdda’s validation flow.

Escrow Release Conditions: Seller funds are released from Escrow to Seller only upon one of the following conditions (whichever occurs first):

  • Successful scan and validation of the Platform Token by an authorized venue scanner (the token is marked "used" by TicketAdda); or
  • Event completion and Buyer confirmation of valid entry within the time period specified in these Terms; or
  • Expiration of a pre-set safe-release period after the Event end if no dispute is raised by Buyer.

If the Buyer raises a dispute per the timeline and procedure in these Terms, funds will remain in Escrow while TicketAdda investigates.

7. Entry Validation & Scanning

  • TicketAdda tokens are intended to be one-time-use. Once a Platform Token is scanned and validated by an authorized scanner, the token is marked "used" and cannot be used again.
  • If a Venue does not accept TicketAdda tokens or if the event organizer requires a provider-issued token only, TicketAdda will attempt to facilitate a provider transfer where supported; otherwise, TicketAdda will disclose the limitation to Buyer prior to purchase.
  • Venue acceptance and enforcement of entry policies are controlled by the event organizer and/or venue; TicketAdda is not the venue operator and does not control venue entry policies.

8. Buyer & Seller Disputes — Reporting & Evidence

  • Reporting Window: A Buyer who is denied entry or who experiences an invalid/used token must report the issue through the Platform’s "Report Entry Issue" flow within 6 hours of the event start time (or such other time window as TicketAdda may publish).
  • Evidence Required: Buyer should provide timestamped evidence where possible (photo, short video of scanner rejection, ticket screenshot, venue staff name/receipt). Seller should provide any evidence of transfer steps taken.
  • Automatic Checks: TicketAdda will run automated checks (token issuance timestamps, token usage timestamps, scanner logs, uploaded original QR hash comparisons, IP/geolocation signals) and may issue an automatic decision if high-confidence indicators of misuse exist.
  • Manual Review: If automated checks are inconclusive, TicketAdda will escalate to manual review and work with the Buyer, Seller, and, where possible, venue operators to resolve the dispute.
  • Resolution Remedies: If TicketAdda determines the Seller misused or fraudulently used the Ticket after sale, TicketAdda will refund Buyer from Escrow, hold Seller funds, and may impose penalties, suspend, or terminate the Seller’s account. If TicketAdda determines Buyer error or venue fault, TicketAdda will act reasonably to remediate (replacement ticket, refund or partial refund) per its policies.
